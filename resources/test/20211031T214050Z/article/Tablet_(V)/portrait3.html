Buoyed by their strong performances in last months 2022 World Cup qualifiers, Malta will be aiming to maintain the positive momentum in their upcoming commitments at home to Slovenia and away to Cyprus.

After taking one point from their opening three Group H qualifiers a 2-2 draw away to Slovakia in March, Malta attained their first win in this World Cup campaign, overpowering Cyprus 3-0 at the National Stadium on 1st September to take their tally to four points. Cain Attards brace and a Joseph Mbong header crowned a commanding display against Cyprus which was followed by another two lively outings in narrow defeats away to Slovenia (0-1) and Russia (0-2).

Malta coach Devis Mangia is pleased with the teams showings in September but, typically, he expects his players to show the same desire and strive to raise the bar further, starting with the home test against Slovenia who only prevailed with a Sandi Lovric penalty in last months encounter in Ljubljana.

I am satisfied with the teams approach in the last three qualifying matches, Mangia said. As I always stress, I prefer to concentrate on the teams performance and, from this point-of-view, we can be satisfied with how we played, even in those matches that yielded no points for us, but we were certainly not happy about the result.

In the last game away to Russia, the team showed great personality while the one against Slovenia was very balanced. We were analysing this match the other day and seeing how we played and the game episodes, especially that dubious penalty, was bittersweet as we deserved better but Id rather look on the positive side. Our performances last month were good but we need to build on that.

Mangia has largely kept faith with the players who were part of the squad for the September internationals but included two new faces in the provisional list, defender James Brown, who turns out for St Johnstone in Scotland, and Floriana midfielder Adam Magri Overend.

James Brown is a young defender who can play in different positions at the back, so that can be useful for us, Mangia said. Im interested to have a closer look at him during this international window.

The selection of Adam Magri Overend is another sign for all the young Maltese players that the doors of the national team are open for everyone. Adam is only 21, he is an interesting prospect who is proving himself in the domestic league and therefore deserves the opportunity to be part of the squad for the upcoming World Cup games.

PHYSICAL CONDITION

With the 2021-22 season gradually gaining momentum, Mangia noted that the physical condition of the players has improved. Generally, the physical condition has improved compared to last month as the players have been involved in more games and subsequently their match fitness is better, Mangia said.

There were a few players who looked slightly tired in the first training sessions but that is normal. The current format of the championship is also having a positive effect in my opinion as the games are balanced and competitive, factors that help to raise the level of performance as the intensity is higher.

With six matches played, the current situation in Group H sees Croatia and Russia lead the pace with 13 points followed by Slovakia on nine. Slovenia, our next opponents, have gathered seven points, three more than Malta and Cyprus.

Looking at the results so far, its clear that the top two teams, Croatia and Russia, have established an advantage in the qualification race, Mangia said. Overall, this has been a well-contested group with many close matches. As I said, Im satisfied with our progress so far but Im very curious to see how we will approach our next two commitments.

What I expect from the team is to keep growing and improving, performance- wise.

HIGH HOPES

Maltas four-point tally coupled with their confident performances of late have raised expectations of getting more points on board and possibly setting a new record in a qualifying campaign for a major competition.

Mangia sounded an upbeat note but he is not getting ahead of himself. We cant allow ourselves to be conditioned by such thoughts, the Italian coach said. We must take one game at a time but the team has certainly shown that we have the qualities to continue on this path. If we manage to keep up and possibly improve our performance levels, in the medium term we can achieve more positive results in terms of points.

Although Mangia and his charges are fully focused on Fridays match against Slovenia, the national coach is wary of the heightened expectations surrounding the away match against Cyprus in Larnaca in three days time.

The next game is always the most important and that will not change, Mangia said. We are focusing all our attention and energies on the Slovenia match but everyone is conscious of the fact that the following assignment is the away clash with Cyprus.

That will be the most difficult match of this campaign because after the result in Malta, Cyprus will be very determined to show that they are a different side compared to that game. We are aware of this and to cope with such scenario, we must perform to the maximum of our abilities, we have to be at our best in terms of attitude and focus because the Cyprus match will be the toughest.
